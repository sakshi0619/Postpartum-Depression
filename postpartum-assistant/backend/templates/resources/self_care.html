Postpartum Self-Care Guide

Daily Self-Care Practices
  • Take 10-minute breathing breaks throughout the day to reduce stress and center yourself.
  • Stay hydrated and eat nutritious meals - your body needs fuel for recovery and caring for your baby.
  • Get 15 minutes of sunlight daily to boost your mood and vitamin D levels.
  • Practice gentle stretching or walking to aid physical recovery and improve circulation.
  • Connect with other new parents to share experiences and reduce feelings of isolation.
Mental Health Tips
  • Be kind to yourself - postpartum recovery takes time and every parent's journey is different.
  • Share your feelings with trusted people who can offer support and understanding.
  • Ask for help when you need it - whether with childcare, household tasks, or emotional support.
  • Remember that mood changes are common but persistent feelings of sadness or anxiety should be discussed with a healthcare provider.
Remember: If you're experiencing persistent sadness, anxiety, or difficulty coping, please reach out to a healthcare provider. Postpartum mood disorders are treatable with professional help.
